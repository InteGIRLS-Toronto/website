About

Our Story

InteGIRLS Founding Team (DC)

INTEGIRLS was first established in Washington DC in 2017 by Joy Shi, a high school student with a mission to allow more young girls and non-binary students comfortable being grouped with girls to explore their interests in math and STEM. As a registered non-profit organization, INTEGIRLS was able to gather more than 130 STEM-passionate students for the INTEGIRLS 2018 Math Contest and more than 15000 students for their 2020 Global Virtual Puzzle Competition.

InteGIRLS Toronto

In 2020, INTEGIRLS was able to spread our impact to other cities, starting new sections and creating more opportunities for girls and non-binary students affected by (trans)misogyny across North America. This was how INTEGIRLS Toronto, Canada's first chapter, was born.

INTEGIRLS began as a group of girls in Washington DC and were able to start sections in other cities across America. Currently, INTEGIRLS Toronto is Canada's first and only chapter, which makes us very unique. If you do not live in the GTA area but would still like to be part of InteGIRLS, you can look into other chapters closer to you or even start your own! Visit integirls.org or their Instagram (@inte.girls) for more information.

There are three divisions for INTEGIRLS math competitions: elementary (grades 5-8), high school (grades 9-12), university (first year +). The competitions at our Toronto chapter are for students in the Greater Toronto Area.

Although only girls and non-binary students comfortable being grouped with girls may participate in our chapter's competitions, they are welcome to volunteer with us and join our executive team.

Most competitions are team-based events for up to 4 students. Participants work together to solve math questions in the math competitions.
